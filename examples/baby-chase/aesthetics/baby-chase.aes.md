# Baby Chase — Playful Discovery

## Experience Goal

The player should feel the delight of finding hidden things in a warm, safe environment.
Each discovery should produce a small burst of surprise and joy — the baby reacts with
expressive emotions (giggling, gasping, hiding again) that make the player feel like
they're interacting with a living character, not solving a puzzle. The world should feel
like a cozy home full of secrets, not a competitive arena.

## Aesthetic Profile

### Primary: Discovery — The joy of finding

The game is framed as "uncharted territory" — every room, every piece of furniture, every
hiding spot is a potential surprise. The player doesn't know where the baby is, and
finding them feels like a genuine discovery, not a task. The baby's unpredictable
reactions (surprise, laughter, playful running away) make each discovery feel unique.

### Secondary: Fantasy — Inhabiting the babysitter role

The player is a babysitter in a playful, slightly exaggerated home. The fantasy isn't
epic — it's the warmth of caring for a child. The baby feels real enough to care about
but cartoonish enough to be unthreatening.

### Secondary: Sensation — Delightful audio-visual responses

Every discovery triggers satisfying visual and audio feedback — the baby's animations,
sound effects, particle bursts. These sensory rewards reinforce the Discovery aesthetic.

### Secondary: Narrative — A bedtime story arc

There is a gentle arc: the baby is awake and playful → you find them → they run away
giggling → you find them again → eventually they get sleepy → you put them to bed.
This gives the experience a beginning, middle, and end without imposing pressure.

## Observable Proxies

- **Discovery rate**: Player finds the baby within 15-45 seconds per hiding spot — Target: median 25s
- **Re-engagement**: After finding the baby, player continues searching within 3s (they want to find them again) — Target: >90% of cycles
- **Emotional reaction time**: Player pauses briefly (0.5-2s) when baby is revealed (moment of delight/surprise) — Target: observable in >60% of discoveries
- **Session completion**: Player completes the full bedtime arc without quitting — Target: >80%
- **Repeat play**: Player starts a new session after completing one — Target: >40%

## Anti-Patterns

- **Frustration search**: Player wanders for >60 seconds without any discovery cue — breaks Discovery, feels aimless instead of exploratory
- **Mechanical repetition**: Baby always hides in the same spots — kills Discovery, turns into rote Challenge
- **Anxiety/pressure**: Any timer, score, or failure state — this is Discovery, not Challenge. Pressure destroys the cozy feeling
- **Passive baby**: Baby doesn't react expressively — without emotional responses, discoveries feel empty (no Sensation payoff)
- **Instant find**: Player finds baby in <5 seconds — no buildup means no discovery moment

## Audience Context

Primary audience is young children (3-7) playing on a parent's device, or playing alongside
a parent. Controls must be extremely simple (tap/click or WASD only). There should be no
text-heavy UI, no fail states, no punishment. The experience should feel like a bedtime
story you play, not a game you win.

This maps to the paper's "First Pass" babysitting game — aesthetics are optimized "not for
winning or competition but for having the baby express emotions like surprise, fear, and
anticipation." Discovery and emotional expression are paramount over Challenge.
